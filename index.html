The Monthly Rotation

The 4-Week SVS Cycle

Once a state qualifies, SVS runs on a repeating monthly rotation. Each week has a defined role — learn it so you know exactly what to stockpile and when.

1
King of Icefield
Week 1 · Mon–Sat

Internal state competition runs as normal. SVS is not active. Focus on everyday growth but begin holding consumables for SVS prep in the following cycle.

2
Sunfire Castle Battle
Week 2 · Saturday

Your state's internal castle battle. Winning alliance controls the SFC and state buffs. The KOI appearing next week signals SVS is approximately two weeks away.

3
SVS Prep Phase
Week 3 · Mon 00:00 – Sat 10:00 UTC

Five and a half days of competitive point scoring. Each day focuses on a different activity. The state with more total points wins and chooses to invade the enemy castle.

4
SVS Battle Phase
Week 4 · Saturday

Cross-state portals open at 10:00 UTC. Castle Battle begins at 12:00 UTC in the losing prep state. Portals stay open until 22:00 UTC. Field Triage opens after.

Calendar Intelligence

How to Predict When SVS Will Arrive

You won't get an exact date far in advance — but these two signals give you reliable advance warning.

📅

Signal #1 — Check the Event Calendar

If King of Icefield (KOI) is scheduled for next week, SVS will arrive approximately 2 weeks from now. This is the clearest advance notice the game gives. Check your in-game event calendar every week and watch for KOI to appear.

🏰

Signal #2 — Count Your Castle Battles

You've just finished your 2nd Sunfire Castle battle. SVS first unlocks around Day 80 of your server. From the current position in the cycle, the state is in the final stretch — matchmaking is imminent. Timing can slip by 1–2 weeks if the system can't find a matched opponent.

💡

While you wait: You don't need to hold everything. Everyday activities (daily quests, gathering, hunting beasts, regular training) are fine. What to stockpile: Fire Crystals, Refined Fire Crystals, Construction/Research Speedups, Training Speedups, Mithril, Hero Shards, Lucky Chips, and Stamina Cans. These are the point-scoring items for specific prep days.

Phase 1 · Monday – Saturday 10:00 UTC

Preparation Phase — Day by Day

5.5 days of competitive scoring against your enemy state. Each day targets a different activity. The state with more total points gets to invade — a massive strategic advantage.

⚠️

Always verify in-game before spending. The in-game Prep Phase panel is the only ground truth for what scores on which day. Specific scoring items can change between SVS events — items in this table were verified against the May 2026 event but may vary in future events. Open the in-game Phases tab before spending any premium item.

⚠️

Holding Upgrades — What Actually Matters: Only hold consumables that directly score points on specific days (Fire Crystals, speedups, Mithril, Hero Shards, etc.). Daily activities like resource gathering, beast hunting with normal stamina, and daily quests are fine to do as usual. You don't need to freeze all progression — just protect your SVS stockpile.

Phase 2 · Saturday 10:00 – 22:00 UTC

Battle Phase — Hour by Hour

The most intense 12 hours in Whiteout Survival. Both states can attack each other simultaneously. Cross-state raids and the Castle Battle happen in parallel.

🚨

Shield Rule: If you are not actively online and fighting during Battle Day, you must be shielded from 10:00 UTC until 22:00 UTC. Enemy players from the opposing state can teleport directly into your hive and attack your city the entire time — even after the Castle Battle ends at ~17:00 UTC.

Cross-state attacks begin		 The inter-state portal activates. Enemy players (Furnace Lv.16+) can now teleport directly into your state — including inside alliance hives — and attack unshielded cities. All president buffs from prep are deactivated. Confirm with R4/R5 which alliance you're joining for the Castle Battle. Don't wait — the window is only 60 minutes.
11:00 Battle PhaseSFC Red Zone Opens Teleportation directly into the Sunfire Castle area of the losing state's territory becomes available. The castle area is "hot" — turrets are live and will attack enemy forces. Castle turrets do NOT attack players from the state that currently controls the SFC. Turrets attack enemy alliance forces. Multiple turret positions surround the main castle. Attacking rallies should be pre-assembled and ready to teleport. Infantry-heavy compositions are used for turret tanking. Rally leaders should have positions marked.
12:00 Battle PhaseCastle Battle Begins
Main event — in the LOSING prep state		 The Sunfire Castle Battle begins in the losing prep state's territory. Alliances rally to capture and hold the castle and its surrounding turrets. This is a territory-control fight. Win condition: Hold the castle for 2.5 consecutive hours, OR accumulate the most total hold time over the full 5-hour battle. Per the Oct 22, 2025 patch, this 2.5-hour win / 5-hour battle rule applies to all servers — including S10+. Only the occupying alliance earns hold-time progress. Attacking formation: ~50% Infantry / 20% Lancer / 30% Marksman. Defending garrison: ~60% Infantry / 40% Lancer / 0% Marksman. Your state's best ralliers should be positioned early.
12:00–17:00 Battle PhaseActive Castle Contest Alliances from both states fight to control the castle. Every troop kill during this period scores points for individuals and alliances. The occupied castle ticks toward the win condition. Points only for kills within ±3 furnace levels of attacker (rally captain's level counts for rally kills). Both states can score individually even if their alliance isn't at the castle. If you're not in the castle fight, enemy raiders are still active in your state. Maintain shields or actively defend. Coordinate with state leaders to split offensive and defensive forces.
~17:00 Castle EndsWinner Declared The Castle Battle concludes when the win condition is met or time expires. The winning alliance's state gains castle control. Supreme President or split Presidents are assigned based on outcomes. See Outcome Scenarios section below for all four result combinations. Castle winner is determined solely by hold time — not troop kills during the battle. Most common mistake: dropping your shield here. The Transfer Portal does NOT close at 17:00. Enemy raiders can still attack your city for 5 more hours. Re-shield immediately.
17:00–22:00 Battle PhasePost-Castle Free Attack Cross-state attacks continue for 5 more hours after the Castle Battle ends. This window is purely for personal and alliance leaderboard point grinding through troop kills. Same ±3 furnace level rule applies. No territory objectives — this is open PvP. Zeroing policies (whether to completely zero cities) are typically agreed upon by state leaders before SVS. Online and aggressive players use this window for personal rankings. Offline or casual players must stay shielded. The enemy state is equally free to raid your cities during this period.
22:00 Event EndsTransfer Portal Closes All cross-state movement ends. SVS scoring is finalized. Battle phase rewards are distributed. Field Triage Phase begins immediately. No more cross-state access after this point. Players who crossed states are automatically returned home or must teleport back. Final leaderboard rankings lock. Collect all battle phase rewards. Count your troop losses. Request or grant Revival Assistance in your alliance. The 90% troop revival window is now open.

Phase 3 · Sat 22:00 – Mon 23:59 UTC

Field Triage — Troop Revival

SVS is the safest large-scale war in the game. Up to 90% of your lost troops can be revived in the 48-hour window after battle ends.

🔄

Base Revival Rate

30%

Automatic baseline revival for all troops lost during SVS battle phase. No action required for this portion.

📜

Rebirth Tomes

+50%

Each player has 20 Rebirth Tomes to give to allies, but can receive up to 50 from other players (+1% per tome). Coordinate with alliance mates so heavy-loss players get tome assistance first.

💎

Revival Potions

+10%

Gem-purchased Revival Potions add up to 10% more (10 potions × 1% each, 2,250 gems total). Combined with tomes, this reaches the maximum revival rate.

🏆

Maximum Revival

90%

30% base + 50% Rebirth Tomes received from allies + 10% Revival Potions = 90%. Note: Healing Speed (+20%) and Healing Capacity buffs are separate mechanics that affect infirmary throughput, not revival rate.

✅

Triage Etiquette: If you lost few troops, wait a day before requesting Rebirth Tomes from alliance mates. Let players with heavy losses get priority assistance first. Coordinate revival order in alliance chat so tomes go where they have the most impact.

Victory Conditions

Outcome Scenarios

Prep and Castle Battle outcomes combine for four possible results. Winning both phases unlocks the most powerful position in the game.

✅ Prep Win · ✅ Castle Win

Supreme President

Your alliance appoints the Supreme President — who holds authority over both states, controlling minister appointments and reward issuance for both. The losing state has no local president for that round (the presidency is vacant). Maximum buffs, maximum prestige. The best possible result.

✅ Prep Win · ❌ Castle Loss

Split Presidents

Each state appoints its own local President. Your SFC was safe (your state was the attacker) but the enemy held the castle. Partial victory.

❌ Prep Loss · ✅ Castle Win

Split Presidents

Your state defended the castle on home turf and won. Each state appoints its own President. An impressive comeback despite losing prep.

❌ Prep Loss · ❌ Castle Loss

Enemy Supreme President

The enemy alliance appoints Supreme President over both states, controlling minister appointments and reward issuance for both. Your state's presidency is vacant for that round — time to rebuild and prepare for the next SVS cycle.

Expert Advice

Pro Tips from the Field

The difference between a state that wins SVS and one that doesn't usually comes down to preparation and coordination, not raw power.

1

Watch for KOI on the Event Calendar

The single most reliable SVS prediction tool. If King of Icefield appears on your event schedule for next week, your SVS battle day is approximately 14 days away. Check the calendar every Sunday to stay ahead of your leadership's holding orders.

2

Hold the Right Things — Not Everything

Fire Crystals, Refined Fire Crystals, Mithril, Training Speedups, Construction Speedups, Research Speedups, Stamina Cans, Mythic/Epic Hero Shards, Lucky Chips, Chief Gear materials. These are the items that score points on specific days. Daily quest completion, normal training, gathering, and beast hunting are perfectly fine to continue.

3

Day 4 Mithril is the Biggest Individual Lever

At 144,000 points per Mithril, a single player spending 10 Mithril contributes 1.44 million prep points. This is 10–20× more efficient than any other single item. If you only stockpile one thing, make it Mithril.

4

Beast Day — Start Rallies, Don't Just Join Them

On Day 3 (Beast Slay), only the rally STARTER scores points from Polar Terror rallies. Players who join get zero points from those kills. Coordinate with your alliance so each member gets turns as rally leader. Don't waste your 30,000-point opportunities by being a perpetual joiner.

5

Shield Up at 09:45 UTC on Battle Saturday

Set a timer. The Transfer Portal opens at exactly 10:00 UTC and enemy players can teleport into your city immediately. If you're not actively fighting, you need to be shielded before that clock hits. A 10-minute buffer is your safety margin.

6

The Castle Battle Ending ≠ SVS Ending

The most common error new SVS players make. The castle fight ends around 17:00 UTC but the Transfer Portal stays open until 22:00 UTC. Players celebrating the battle by dropping their shield get zeroed. Re-shield the moment the castle result is announced and don't drop it until 22:00 UTC.

7

Don't Fear Troop Loss — SVS Has 90% Revival

SVS is the most forgiving combat event for troop loss. With alliance coordination on Rebirth Tomes and the built-in revival system, players can recover up to 90% of losses. Fight aggressively — the troop loss risk is far lower than a normal war. Passivity loses states more than reckless fighting does.

8

Gatherers on Tiles at 16:00 UTC Monday

Sending gathering marches out at 16:00 UTC on Day 1 (Monday) times them to return on Tuesday morning — right as Day 2 (Basic Skills Up) begins. Gathering scores points when you collect from tiles, so returning marches at Day 2 start maximizes your gathering contribution without wasting Day 1 scoring time.

9

Don't Alliance-Hop Before Tally

Alliance rewards are not distributed to members who leave the alliance before the tally phase. If you're considering changing alliances, wait until after SVS rewards are issued — leaving early means forfeiting your share of alliance leaderboard rewards, ranking rewards, and tier bonuses. This catches new players off guard most often.
